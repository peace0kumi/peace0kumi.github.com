BKL’s Antitrust and Competition Practice Wins "Behavioural Matter of the Year" Award

2017-04-07
BKL was named the winner of the “Behavioural Matter of the Year – Asia-Pacific, Middle East and Africa” award for its representation of global electronics giants in investigations by the Korea Fair Trade Commission (“KFTC”) of Qualcomm at the 7th Annual GCR Awards held on March 28, 2017 in Washington D.C. Global Competition Review (“GCR”) is the world’s leading antitrust and competition law journal based in the U.K. Annual GCR Awards are given to distinguished lawfirms, competition authorities, cases and experts every year through online voting. BKL received the coveted “Behavioural Matter of the Year” award for advising Apple, Samsung Electronics and LG Electronics on “Qualcomm’s abuse of its dominant position in patent licensing.” The KFTC imposed the largest ever fine on Qualcomm and received the “Agency of the Year” award, among others, for its enforcement action against Qualcomm, which has drawn widespread attention from around the world. The Antitrust and Competition Team of BKL comprises more than 30 professionals, including former KFTC officials, former judges and talented attorneys with extensive theoretical and practical knowledge of antitrust and competition laws. The team also has unparalleled expertise in antitrust and competition matters and provides comprehensive legal services from the KFTC administrative investigation stage up to representation in court procedures. In addition, former KFTC commissioner Joong Weon JEONG joined BKL in December last year to further enhance BKL’s antitrust and competition practice. Before joining BKL, Mr. JEONG served as the vice chairman of the Competition Committee under the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (“OECD”) and held key positions at the KFTC.
