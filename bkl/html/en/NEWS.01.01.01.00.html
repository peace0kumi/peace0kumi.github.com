BKL Advises on the Republic of Korea’s issuance of Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds

2017-04-04

BKL advised the Republic of Korea on its successfu issuance of US$1,000,000,000 2.75% foreign exchange equalization bonds due 2027 (the "Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds") on January 19, 2017. The Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds were listed on the Singapore Exchange.

The Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds are the first US dollar denominated bonds issued by the Republic of Korea since its credit ratings were upgraded to AA by S&P and has received high interest from investors with initial orders coming in triple the amount being sold from approximately 70 institutions across Asia, Europe and US regions. The interest rate for the Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds, being US 10 year treasury bonds + 55bp which is low compared to those of bonds issued by issuers with similar level of credit ratings, and is the lowest interest rate since the Republic of Korea started issuing US dollar denominated foreign exchange equalization bonds. This successful Foreign Exchange Equalization Bond issuance has been regarded as a transaction showing investors’ confidence in the Korean economy and is expected to be a benchmark for future foreign currency denominated bonds by Korean issuers, creating a positive setting for issuance of overseas bonds by other Korean institutions.

BKL’s team, comprising of partner Eui Jong (EJ) CHUNG, attorneys Seungil HONG, Youngjin HA and Jung Won HWANG, senior foreign attorney Annie Eunah LEE and foreign attorney Heesug CHUNG, provided comprehensive Korean legal advisory services in connection with the Republic of Korea’s Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds issuance, including reviewing the registration statement and prospectus filed with the US SEC, reviewing related agreements and documents, issuing legal opinions to relevant US regulators and the underwriters, and reviewing and advising on Korean law issues relating to the transaction overall. In particular, BKL played an important role in drafting the descriptions in the prospectus relating to the complicated political situation at the time of the issuance of the Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds to assist foreign investors’ understanding of the situation.

In connection with this transaction, Citigroup Global Markets, Goldman Sachs International, HSBC Limited, J.P. Morgan Securities, Merrill Lynch, The Korea Development Bank and Samsung Securities Co., Ltd. acted as initial purchasers for the Foreign Exchange Equalization Bonds, Cleary, Gottlieb, Steen & Hamilton LLP acted as US counsel to the Republic of Korea and Simpson Thacher & Bartlett LLP acted as US counsel to the initial purchasers.
